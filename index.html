About

Hi there.

My Name is Nathan Hall. I am a Software Engineer with a background in business and leadership. I am skilled in both technical and strategic aspects of business and am developing related skills in software development. I am completing the Full-Stack Web Developer program at Holberton Tulsa, building on my experiencing developing lead generation websites for local businesses. I have valuable experience in customer relations, sales, and team management in leadership and operations management roles in former housing and military careers. I am seeking a position that will allow me to gain experience building software solutions for businesses and brands.

My professional user experience with business and military software systems piqued my interest in web development. Modern operations, whether in Tulsa or Kabul, rely on software solutions that are either unusuable, laden with tech debt, or inaccessible to businesses and brands who may need them. I want to build experience with UX, web app architecture, and open source development to be part of solutions to these common problems.

I was raised by the internet and have seen it evolve through Web 1.0 and Web 2.0. I have seen the rise of global platforms like Facebook and LinkedIn force the way humans communicate into a pattern that serves the vision of the few over the needs of the people. We are at a crossroads in the history of the internet where the decentralization of communication technology can enable genuine human communication and consensus. Although no one knows exactly what the evolution into Web 3.0 will look like, there is already a fight over who owns and benefits from the restructuring of the peoples' most valuable communications asset: our internet. My primary goal is to build the skills that will enable me to contribute to a team that is leveraging technology to enable authentic, equitable democracy and retake ownership of human communication through the internet.
